# Diabetes Risk Factors — Comprehensive Reference

Source: ADA Standards of Care 2026, CDC, WHO

---

## Type 2 Diabetes Risk Factors

### Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

| Factor | Detail | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Age | >= 35 years (risk increases with age) | ~14% of adults 18+ have diabetes globally |
| Family history | First-degree relative with type 2 diabetes | 2-6x increased risk |
| Race/ethnicity | African American, Hispanic/Latino, Native American, Asian American, Pacific Islander | 1.5-2x higher prevalence |
| History of gestational diabetes | Previous GDM diagnosis | 50% develop type 2 within 5-10 years |
| Polycystic ovary syndrome | PCOS diagnosis | Insulin resistance central to pathology |
| History of cardiovascular disease | Prior MI, stroke, or peripheral vascular disease | Shared metabolic pathways |

### Modifiable Risk Factors

| Factor | Threshold | Impact |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Overweight/obesity | BMI >= 25 kg/m² (>= 23 for Asian Americans) | Primary modifiable risk factor |
| Abdominal obesity | Waist >40 in (men), >35 in (women) | Visceral fat drives insulin resistance |
| Physical inactivity | <150 min/week moderate activity | Sedentary lifestyle increases risk 2x |
| Unhealthy diet | High sugar, refined carbs, saturated fat | Directly impacts glucose metabolism |
| Smoking | Current or recent smoking | 30-40% increased risk vs non-smokers |
| Hypertension | >= 140/90 mmHg | Shared metabolic syndrome component |
| Dyslipidemia | HDL <35 mg/dL or triglycerides >250 mg/dL | Indicator of metabolic dysfunction |
| Prediabetes | A1C 5.7-6.4%, FPG 100-125 mg/dL | 15-30% progress to diabetes in 5 years |
| Sleep disorders | Obstructive sleep apnea, <6 hrs sleep | Worsens insulin resistance |
| Stress | Chronic psychological stress | Cortisol elevates blood glucose |

### Environmental/Socioeconomic Factors
- Food insecurity and limited access to healthy foods
- Limited healthcare access
- Low health literacy
- Neighborhood walkability and safety
- Environmental pollutant exposure

## Type 1 Diabetes Risk Factors

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Genetic susceptibility | HLA-DR3, HLA-DR4 genes; family history (5-6% if parent/sibling affected) |
| Autoantibodies | Presence of islet cell autoantibodies (ICA, anti-GAD, anti-IA-2, anti-ZnT8) |
| Geographic location | Higher incidence in Northern European countries (Finland, Sweden) |
| Viral infections | Possible triggers: enteroviruses, Coxsackie B |
| Infant nutrition | Early introduction of cow's milk, gluten (research ongoing) |
| Vitamin D deficiency | Lower levels associated with higher risk |

## Gestational Diabetes Risk Factors

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Overweight/obesity | BMI >= 25 before pregnancy |
| Age | Over 25 years |
| Family history | Diabetes in first-degree relative |
| Previous GDM | History of gestational diabetes |
| Previous macrosomia | Previous baby weighing >9 lbs (4.1 kg) |
| Race/ethnicity | African American, Hispanic, Native American, Asian, Pacific Islander |
| PCOS | Polycystic ovary syndrome diagnosis |
| Physical inactivity | Sedentary lifestyle before/during pregnancy |

## Risk Assessment for Triage

### High Risk (Should Be Screened Annually)
- Prediabetes (A1C 5.7-6.4%)
- Previous gestational diabetes
- BMI >= 30 with any additional risk factor
- Multiple risk factors present

### Moderate Risk (Screen Every 3 Years)
- Age >= 35 with no other risk factors
- Overweight with 1 risk factor
- Family history only

### Low Risk
- No known risk factors
- Normal weight
- Under 35 with no family history

## Risk Factor Scoring for Triage Agent

These factors amplify the risk score when present alongside active symptoms:

| Factor | Score Impact | Rationale |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Age > 60 | +0.10 | Higher complication risk, atypical presentations |
| Known diabetes + new symptoms | +0.15 | May indicate decompensation or complication |
| Medication non-adherence | +0.10 | Higher risk of acute events |
| Multiple symptoms (>= 3) | +0.10 | Suggests more advanced condition |
| Duration > 2 weeks | +0.10 | Untreated symptoms may worsen |
| Family history of diabetes | +0.05 | Increased baseline risk |
| Pregnancy + symptoms | +0.10 | Risk to mother and fetus |

Maximum combined amplifier contribution: 0.30 (prevents over-escalation)

## Prevention Evidence

### Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) Findings
- Lifestyle intervention reduced type 2 diabetes incidence by 58%
- In adults 60+, reduction was 71%
- Key components:
  - 5-7% body weight loss
  - 150 minutes/week of moderate activity
  - Healthy eating changes
- Benefits persisted for 15+ years after intervention
- Metformin reduced incidence by 31% (most effective in younger, higher-BMI individuals)

### WHO Recommendations
- Maintain healthy body weight
- Exercise 150+ minutes per week at moderate intensity
- Eat a healthy diet avoiding sugar and saturated fat
- Avoid tobacco

## Disclaimer

Risk factor presence does not mean a person has or will develop diabetes. Only proper medical testing can diagnose diabetes. This information supports triage assessment and should not replace clinical evaluation.
