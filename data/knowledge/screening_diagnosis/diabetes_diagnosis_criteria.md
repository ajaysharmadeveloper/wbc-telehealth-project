# Diabetes Diagnosis Criteria

Source: ADA Standards of Care 2026, WHO Guidelines

---

## Diagnostic Tests and Thresholds

### A1C (Glycated Hemoglobin)
- **Diabetes**: A1C >= 6.5%
- **Prediabetes**: A1C 5.7% to 6.4%
- **Normal**: A1C < 5.7%
- Reflects average blood glucose over the past 2-3 months
- Does not require fasting

### Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG)
- **Diabetes**: FPG >= 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- **Prediabetes (Impaired Fasting Glucose)**: FPG 100-125 mg/dL (5.6-6.9 mmol/L)
- **Normal**: FPG < 100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L)
- Requires 8-hour fast before testing

### Oral Glucose Tolerance Test (OGTT)
- **Diabetes**: 2-hour plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)
- **Prediabetes (Impaired Glucose Tolerance)**: 2-hour plasma glucose 140-199 mg/dL (7.8-11.0 mmol/L)
- **Normal**: 2-hour plasma glucose < 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L)
- Measured 2 hours after 75g glucose load

### Random Plasma Glucose
- **Diabetes**: Random glucose >= 200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) WITH classic hyperglycemic symptoms
- Classic symptoms: polyuria, polydipsia, unexplained weight loss
- Does not require fasting or glucose load

## Confirming Diagnosis
- In the absence of unequivocal hyperglycemia, diagnosis requires TWO abnormal test results from the same sample or from two separate test samples
- If two different tests are performed and both are above diagnostic thresholds, diabetes is confirmed
- If results are discordant, repeat the test that is above the diagnostic threshold

## Who Should Be Screened

### Adults (ADA Recommendations)
- All adults aged 35 and older, regardless of weight
- Adults of any age who are overweight/obese AND have one or more additional risk factors:
  - First-degree relative with diabetes
  - High-risk race/ethnicity (African American, Latino, Native American, Asian American, Pacific Islander)
  - History of cardiovascular disease
  - Hypertension (>= 140/90 mmHg or on therapy)
  - HDL cholesterol < 35 mg/dL and/or triglycerides > 250 mg/dL
  - Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  - Physical inactivity
  - A1C >= 5.7%, impaired glucose tolerance, or impaired fasting glucose on previous testing
  - History of gestational diabetes
  - HIV infection

### Pregnancy Screening
- Test for undiagnosed diabetes at first prenatal visit (risk-based)
- Screen ALL pregnant women for gestational diabetes at 24-28 weeks gestation
- Screen women with gestational diabetes for prediabetes/diabetes at 4-12 weeks postpartum
- Lifelong screening every 1-3 years for women with history of gestational diabetes

### Children and Adolescents
- Screen youth who are overweight (BMI >= 85th percentile) or obese AND have one or more risk factors:
  - Maternal diabetes or gestational diabetes during child's gestation
  - Family history of type 2 diabetes in first or second-degree relative
  - High-risk race/ethnicity
  - Signs of insulin resistance (acanthosis nigricans, hypertension, dyslipidemia, PCOS)

## Screening Frequency
- If results are normal, repeat testing at minimum 3-year intervals
- More frequent testing may be appropriate based on initial results and risk status
- Annual testing recommended for patients with prediabetes

## Diabetes Types

### Type 1 Diabetes
- Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells
- Absolute insulin deficiency
- Usually diagnosed in children/young adults but can occur at any age
- Markers: islet cell autoantibodies (ICA), anti-GAD, anti-IA-2, anti-ZnT8
- Requires insulin therapy from diagnosis

### Type 2 Diabetes
- Progressive loss of adequate beta-cell insulin secretion
- Frequently on the background of insulin resistance
- Accounts for 90-95% of all diabetes
- Often preceded by years of prediabetes
- May be managed initially with lifestyle changes and oral medications

### Gestational Diabetes
- Diabetes diagnosed in the second or third trimester of pregnancy
- Not clearly pre-existing type 1 or type 2 diabetes
- Increases risk of future type 2 diabetes for both mother and child
- Usually resolves after delivery

### Other Specific Types
- Monogenic diabetes syndromes (neonatal diabetes, MODY)
- Diseases of the exocrine pancreas (cystic fibrosis, pancreatitis)
- Drug or chemical-induced diabetes (glucocorticoids, HIV treatment, organ transplant)
